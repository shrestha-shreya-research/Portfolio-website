About Me

Background & Experience

I am a Housing Specialist at the Housing Assistance Council, advocating for capacity building through training and technical assistance in rural affordable housing. My work sits at the intersection of housing policy and rural community sustainability, with a particular focus on how targeted housing interventions influence neighborhood stability and moderate-income household retention, drawing on spatial analysis, statistical modeling, and GIS-based approaches.

Prior to this role, I earned a Master's degree in City and Metropolitan Planning from the University of Utah and worked as a GIS Analyst at the Utah Center for Civic Improvement, where I contributed to projects mapping ADUs in Salt Lake City. My work has been shaped by collaboration with nonprofit organizations, local governments, and community stakeholders to support equitable and sustainable housing solutions.

Outside of my professional work, I am committed to community engagement and translating policy research into actionable strategies. I believe strongly in evidence-based planning that strengthens rural communities and ensures vulnerable populations have access to safe, stable, and sustainable housing.
